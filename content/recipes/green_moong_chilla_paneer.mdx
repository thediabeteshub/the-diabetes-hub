## ğŸ‘©â€ğŸ³ Step-by-Step Instructions

### **Step 1: Prepare the Moong (4 Hours Ahead)**
Rinse green moong thoroughly and soak in plenty of water for 4-6 hours or overnight. *Pro tip: Use warm water to reduce soaking time to 3 hours if you're in a hurry.*

### **Step 2: Create the Perfect Batter**
Drain soaked moong and add to a blender with ginger, green chilies, and Â¼ cup water. Grind to a smooth, thick paste (consistency should be like pancake batter). Add salt, cumin seeds, and chopped cilantro. Mix well and let rest for 10 minutes.

### **Step 3: Prepare the Paneer Stuffing**
Heat 1 tsp oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle. Add diced onions and cook until translucent (3-4 minutes). Add green chilies, turmeric, and cook for 1 minute. Add chopped tomatoes and all spices. Cook until tomatoes break down (4-5 minutes). Add crumbled paneer and cilantro, mix gently, and cook for 2-3 minutes. Set aside to cool slightly.

### **Step 4: Test Your Batter Consistency**
The batter should be pourable but not too thin. If it's too thick, add water 1 tablespoon at a time. If too thin, let it rest for 15 minutes to thicken naturally.

### **Step 5: Cook the First Chilla**
Heat a non-stick pan or well-seasoned cast iron tawa over medium heat. Lightly oil the surface. Pour Â¼ cup batter in center and quickly spread in a circular motion using the back of a ladle. Cook for 2-3 minutes until edges lift slightly and bottom is golden.

### **Step 6: Add the Stuffing**
Place 2-3 tablespoons of paneer mixture on one half of the chilla while it's still on the pan. The residual heat will warm the filling perfectly.

### **Step 7: Fold and Finish**
Gently fold the chilla in half, press lightly, and cook for another minute. The paneer should be warm and slightly melted. Slide onto serving plate.

### **Step 8: Repeat and Serve**
Continue with remaining batter, adding oil as needed. Serve immediately while hot with your favorite chutney or yogurt.

## â“ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use split moong dal instead of whole moong?**
A: Absolutely! Split moong dal works well and actually soaks faster (2-3 hours). The texture will be slightly smoother but equally delicious.

**Q: My chilla is breaking when I try to fold it. What's wrong?**
A: This usually means the batter is too thin or the pan temperature is too high. Try thickening the batter and reducing heat slightly.

**Q: Can I make this vegan?**
A: Yes! Replace paneer with crumbled tofu or vegan paneer. Season the tofu well with nutritional yeast for a cheesy flavor.

**Q: How do I prevent the chilla from sticking?**
A: Use a well-seasoned cast iron pan or good non-stick pan, ensure proper oil distribution, and maintain medium heat throughout cooking.

**Q: Can I prepare the batter in advance?**
A: Yes, the batter actually improves after sitting for a few hours. Store in refrigerator for up to 3 days, stirring well before each use.

**Q: Is this safe for people with diabetes?**
A: Yes, this is an excellent choice for diabetics due to its low glycemic index and high protein content, which helps stabilize blood sugar.
