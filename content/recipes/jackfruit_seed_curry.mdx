## ğŸ‘©â€ğŸ³ Step-by-Step Instructions

### **Step 1: Prepare the Jackfruit Seeds (15 mins)**
1. **Boil the seeds:** Place jackfruit seeds in a pressure cooker with enough water to cover. Pressure cook for 2 whistles, or boil in a regular pot for 12-15 minutes until tender when pierced with a fork.

2. **Peel while warm:** Drain and let cool slightly. While still warm (easier to peel!), remove the outer white skin and inner brown membrane. The peeled seed should be smooth and ivory-colored.

3. **Optional roasting:** Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a pan and lightly roast the peeled seeds for 3-4 minutes until golden spots appear. This adds incredible depth of flavor!

### **Step 2: Make the Coconut Base (10 mins)**
4. **Coconut paste method:** If using fresh coconut, blend with Â½ cup warm water until smooth. Strain to extract thick coconut milk. *Skip if using canned coconut milk.*

5. **SautÃ© aromatics:** Heat 2 tablespoons coconut oil in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add chopped onions and cook until golden (5-6 minutes). Add ginger, garlic, and green chilies. SautÃ© for 2 minutes until fragrant.

### **Step 3: Build the Curry (15 mins)**
6. **Add spices:** Lower heat and add turmeric, chili powder, and coriander powder. Stir for 30 seconds until aromatic (don't let them burn!).

7. **Create the gravy:** Pour in coconut milk gradually, stirring constantly. Add the roasted jackfruit seeds and bring to a gentle simmer.

8. **Simmer and season:** Let it bubble gently for 10-12 minutes until the seeds absorb the flavors and the curry reaches your desired consistency. Season with salt.

### **Step 4: The Perfect Tempering (3 mins)**
9. **Final tadka:** In a small pan, heat remaining oil. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add curry leaves and sliced shallots. Fry until shallots are golden.

10. **Finish and serve:** Pour this tempering over the curry. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot!

## â“ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Are jackfruit seeds safe to eat?**
A: Absolutely! Jackfruit seeds are not only safe but highly nutritious. Just make sure to cook them thoroughly â€” never eat them raw.

**Q: How do I know when the seeds are properly cooked?**
A: They should be tender enough to pierce easily with a fork, similar to a boiled potato. The cooking time varies with seed size.

**Q: Can I use canned/packaged jackfruit instead?**
A: This recipe specifically uses fresh jackfruit seeds, which have a completely different texture than canned jackfruit flesh. Stick with fresh seeds for authentic results.

**Q: What if I can't find curry leaves?**
A: While curry leaves add authentic flavor, you can substitute with bay leaves (remove before serving) or simply skip them. The curry will still be delicious!

**Q: Is this curry spicy?**
A: The heat level is easily adjustable! Start with less chili powder and green chilies, then add more to taste. The coconut milk helps balance any heat.

**Q: How long do boiled jackfruit seeds last?**
A: Peeled, cooked seeds stay fresh in the refrigerator for 3-4 days or can be frozen for up to 6 months.
