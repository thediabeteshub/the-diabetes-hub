## 🥬 Ingredients (Makes 8-10 Theplas)

**🌾 Whole Wheat Flour**
➡️ **Quantity:** 2 cups (240g)

**🍃 Fresh Fenugreek Leaves (Methi)**
➡️ **Quantity:** 1 cup finely chopped (about 50g)

**🌰 Ground Flax Seeds**
➡️ **Quantity:** 3 tablespoons

**🧈 Plain Yogurt (or water for vegan)**
➡️ **Quantity:** 1/4 cup

**🧄 Ginger-Green Chili Paste**
➡️ **Quantity:** 1 tablespoon

**🌶️ Turmeric Powder**
➡️ **Quantity:** 1/2 teaspoon

**🌶️ Red Chili Powder**
➡️ **Quantity:** 1/2 teaspoon (adjust to taste)

**🌿 Cumin Powder**
➡️ **Quantity:** 1/2 teaspoon

**🌿 Coriander Powder**
➡️ **Quantity:** 1/2 teaspoon

**🧂 Salt**
➡️ **Quantity:** 1 teaspoon (or to taste)

**🫒 Oil**
➡️ **Quantity:** 2 tablespoons (for dough) + extra for cooking

**💧 Water**
➡️ **Quantity:** As needed for kneading

## 👩‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

### **Step 1: Prep Your Methi Like a Pro**
Start by washing the fresh fenugreek leaves thoroughly – I mean *really* thoroughly. These little leaves love to hide dirt! Pat them completely dry with paper towels, then chop them finely. Pro tip: slightly bitter methi leaves actually work better for theplas, so don't worry if they're not super sweet.

### **Step 2: Create Your Superfood Flour Mix**
In a large mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, ground flax seeds, turmeric, red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well with a fork to ensure even distribution. The flax seeds will add a subtle nutty flavor and incredible nutritional value.

### **Step 3: Build Flavor Layers**
Add the chopped methi leaves, ginger-green chili paste, yogurt (or water for vegan), and 2 tablespoons of oil to your flour mixture. Mix everything together with your hands – this is where the magic happens. The leaves will release their oils and the spices will bloom.

### **Step 4: Knead to Perfection**
Gradually add water, a tablespoon at a time, while kneading. You want a firm but pliable dough – not too soft or the theplas will be difficult to roll. The dough should feel slightly tacky but not sticky. Let it rest for 10 minutes covered with a damp cloth.

### **Step 5: Master the Roll**
Divide the dough into 8-10 equal portions. On a lightly floured surface, roll each portion into a thin circle (about 7-8 inches diameter). Don't worry about perfect circles – rustic shapes have more character! The key is even thickness for uniform cooking.

### **Step 6: Cook to Golden Perfection**
Heat a tava or heavy-bottomed skillet over medium heat. Place a thepla on the hot surface and cook for 1-2 minutes until you see bubbles forming. Flip and cook the other side for another minute. Brush lightly with oil and cook until golden brown spots appear. The aroma should be absolutely irresistible by now!

### **Step 7: Keep Them Warm and Soft**
Stack the cooked theplas in a clean kitchen towel or roti container to keep them warm and soft. This step is crucial – it prevents them from becoming dry and brittle.

## 💡 Pro Tips & Genius Variations

### **🎯 Cooking Hacks That Change Everything**
- **Perfect Dough Test:** The dough is ready when it springs back lightly after poking
- **Oil Strategy:** Brush oil only after the first flip for healthier theplas
- **Heat Control:** Medium heat is your friend – too high and they'll burn before cooking through
- **Storage Secret:** Cool completely before storing to prevent condensation

### **🌈 Delicious Variations to Try**
- **Extra Protein:** Add 2 tbsp besan (chickpea flour) for more protein
- **Gluten-Free:** Replace wheat flour with a mix of besan and millet flour
- **Spice It Up:** Add a pinch of hing (asafoetida) and ajwain (carom seeds)
- **Green Goddess:** Blend in some spinach for extra iron and vibrant color

### **⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid**
- Don't skip the resting time – it makes rolling much easier
- Avoid over-kneading which can make theplas tough
- Don't roll too thick or they won't cook properly inside
- Never store warm theplas – they'll become soggy

## 📦 Storage & Meal Prep Magic

**🏠 Room Temperature:** Store in an airtight container for up to 2 days
**❄️ Refrigerator:** Keep fresh for up to 5 days wrapped in aluminum foil
**🧊 Freezer:** Freeze for up to 1 month with parchment paper between each thepla

**⚡ Quick Reheat Methods:**
- Tava: 30 seconds each side for that fresh-made taste
- Microwave: 20-30 seconds wrapped in damp paper towel
- Toaster: Light setting for a slightly crispy texture

## ❓ Your Questions Answered

**Q: My theplas are turning out tough. What am I doing wrong?**
A: This usually happens from over-kneading the dough or using too much flour while rolling. Keep the dough slightly soft and use minimal flour for dusting.

**Q: Can I make these without yogurt for a vegan version?**
A: Absolutely! Replace yogurt with the same amount of water. You might need to add an extra tablespoon of oil to maintain moisture.

**Q: I don't have fresh methi leaves. Can I use dried ones?**
A: Fresh is best, but you can use 2 tablespoons of dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi). Soak them in warm water for 5 minutes, then drain and use.

**Q: Are these suitable for diabetics?**
A: Yes! The combination of whole wheat flour, fenugreek, and flax seeds helps regulate blood sugar levels. However, portion control is still important.

**Q: Can I prepare the dough ahead of time?**
A: Yes, you can prepare the dough up to 4 hours in advance. Cover tightly and refrigerate. Let it come to room temperature before rolling.

**Q: Why are my theplas not puffing up?**
A: Theplas typically don't puff like regular rotis due to the added ingredients. This is completely normal and doesn't affect the taste or nutrition.
