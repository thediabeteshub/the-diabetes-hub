## ğŸ‘©â€ğŸ³ Step-by-Step Instructions

### **Step 1: Prep the Moong Dal (5 minutes)**
Rinse the moong dal under cold water until the water runs clear. No soaking required â€” that's the beauty of split moong dal!

### **Step 2: Roast to Golden Perfection (8-10 minutes)**
Heat 1 tablespoon of ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Add the washed moong dal and roast, stirring constantly, until it turns golden and releases a nutty aroma. **Pro tip:** The dal should sound like popcorn when it's properly roasted!

### **Step 3: Cook the Dal (15 minutes)**
Add 2 cups of water and a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the dal is completely soft and mashable. The water should be mostly absorbed.

### **Step 4: Mash and Transform (3 minutes)**
Using a potato masher or the back of a spoon, mash the cooked dal until smooth. Don't worry about getting it perfectly smooth â€” a little texture adds character!

### **Step 5: Add the Magic (12-15 minutes)**
Add the remaining 3 tablespoons of ghee to the pan. Stir in the mashed dal and cook on medium-low heat, stirring continuously. Gradually add the milk, one cup at a time, letting each addition cook down before adding the next.

### **Step 6: Sweeten & Spice (5 minutes)**
Once the mixture thickens and starts pulling away from the sides of the pan, add your chosen sweetener and cardamom powder. Stir well and cook for another 2-3 minutes until the sweetener is fully incorporated.

### **Step 7: Final Flourish**
Garnish with chopped nuts and serve warm or at room temperature.

## â“ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use jaggery instead of artificial sweeteners?**
A: While jaggery is more natural, it will raise the glycemic index significantly. If you're not diabetic and prefer natural sweeteners, use 1/3 cup jaggery, but this won't be sugar-free anymore.

**Q: My halwa turned out too thin. How can I fix it?**
A: Continue cooking on low heat, stirring constantly, until more moisture evaporates. You can also mix 1 tablespoon of ghee with 1 teaspoon of besan (chickpea flour) and stir it in.

**Q: Can I make this without ghee?**
A: Yes! Use coconut oil for a vegan version, or even butter will work. The flavor will be slightly different but still delicious.

**Q: How do I know when the halwa is ready?**
A: The halwa is done when it starts leaving the sides of the pan and you can draw a line through it with your spatula that holds for a few seconds.

**Q: Is this really suitable for diabetics?**
A: This recipe uses sugar substitutes with low glycemic impact, but portion control is still important. Always consult with your healthcare provider about including any dessert in your meal plan.
